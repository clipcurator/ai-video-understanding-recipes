# Narrative Extraction Checklist

Narrative extraction helps convert a long story-driven video into a shorter recap, trailer, or highlight sequence without losing the basic story logic.

## Story elements

- Who is the central character?
- What does the character want?
- What blocks the character?
- What changes during the story?
- What moment creates the strongest emotional turn?
- What information must be preserved for the audience to understand the ending?

## Beat map

| Beat | Question | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Setup | What world, problem, or relationship is introduced? |  |
| Inciting moment | What starts the main movement of the story? |  |
| Escalation | What makes the situation harder? |  |
| Reveal | What changes the audience's understanding? |  |
| Climax | What is the highest-stakes moment? |  |
| Resolution | What is the outcome or takeaway? |  |

## Editing risks

- The clip starts after the audience needs context.
- The villain, goal, or stakes are unclear.
- A later reveal is used before the setup.
- The recap compresses events but loses causality.
- The emotional peak is shown without buildup.

## Output format

